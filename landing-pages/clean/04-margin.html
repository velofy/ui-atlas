Editor's note

A note from the gutter

Every page has a margin, the quiet strip beside the argument where a reader talks back. We named the journal for that space, because that is where reading turns into thinking: the underline, the objection, the "yes, but" pencilled in past midnight.

Most of what you read now is built to be finished and forgotten. We are building the opposite. Four times a year we print essays long enough to change your mind, and slow enough to let you. Then we leave the margin wide and wait for you to fill it.

So read with a pencil. Argue in the gutter. Then send the pages back, because the sharpest notes get set in type.
