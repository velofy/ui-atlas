Our position

Electric should feel like progress you can feel in your spine, not a number you read on a screen. So we built a car that plugs in and still refuses to disappear.

Three things we
refused to fake

Range, charging, and feel are where most electric cars quietly cut corners. These are the three we would not.

Range

540 km, measured honestly

We quote range with the heating on, the highway under you, and five people aboard. The number you see is the number you get, not a best-case figure from a climate-controlled lab at 23 degrees.

Charging

10 to 80 in 18 minutes

A 350 kW pack that holds its charge rate instead of tapering the second you plug in. Long enough to stretch your legs, short enough that you resent the coffee line more than the car.

Drive

Torque that reads the corner

Dual rear-biased motors and adaptive vectoring send power to the wheel that wants it, mid-bend, in real time. It does not correct your line. It rewards it.
