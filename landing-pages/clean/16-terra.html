A point of view

Averages are not evidence.

Most carbon tools multiply what you spent by an industry average and hand you a number. It looks precise. It cannot survive a single hard question. Terra takes the other road: we measure from the activity itself, so the footprint you publish is the footprint you can prove.

The old way: spend-based

A guess dressed up as a figure

Forty thousand dollars of freight, multiplied by a national average factor. Change carriers, change routes, cut mileage in half, and the number does not move.

$40,000 freight spend x 0.52 kg/$ average = ~20.8 t CO2e (unverifiable)
