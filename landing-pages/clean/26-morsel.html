The Morsel idea

Great food should not have to travel far.

We built Morsel around one rule: if a kitchen cannot reach you hot on a bike, it does not belong in your feed. That single rule changes everything downstream, from how fast your dinner lands to how much your neighbors take home.

Only the kitchens nearby

Every kitchen on Morsel is an independent spot within a 12-minute ride of your address. No national chains, no far-off ghost kitchens cooking for ten apps at once.

Independent only

Hot noodles, cold drinks

Short rides mean short trips. Median delivery is under 18 minutes, so your noodles stay springy and your iced tea reaches you still cold, not lukewarm.

Under 18 min median

Fair pay, close to home

Riders keep every cent of their tips and earn a guaranteed minimum on each trip. Kitchens pay one flat monthly fee, never a slice of every single plate they sell.

Flat fee, full tips

How the radius works

One short ride, start to finish.

When you open Morsel, you only see what can actually reach you hot. Everything inside the ring is on the menu. Everything past it, chains included, stays off your screen entirely.

  1. 1 Drop your pinWe draw a 12-minute ride around your address and quietly hide everything beyond it.
