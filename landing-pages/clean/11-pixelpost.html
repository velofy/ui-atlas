Why it feels different

Built for the good part of the old web

We kept the parts that made the early internet fun to be on, and left the ad auctions, the rage bait, and the engagement math where they belong: in the recycle bin.

Strictly chronological

Newest at the top, older below, and nothing in between quietly reordered to keep you scrolling. What your friends posted is exactly what you see, in the order they posted it.

no ranking, no reshuffling

Circles, not audiences

Every circle caps at 100 people. Small enough to remember names, big enough for a real party. Follower counts were never the point, and here there is nothing to count.

capped at 100 on purpose

Make it yours again

Chrome themes, sparkle cursors, an autoplay blingtone that greets visitors, and a guestbook they can sign. Your page should look like you, not like one more identical template.

themes, cursors, guestbooks

Your page, fully chromed

A profile that actually feels like a place

Remember when your page was a room you decorated, not a card in a stack? Pixel Post brings that back, with modern bones underneath so it loads fast and reads clean.

  • Pick a chrome theme or build your own gradient from two colors.
  • Set a blingtone that plays once when a friend drops by. Off by default, always mutable.
  • Keep a guestbook so visits leave a trace instead of a silent view count.
